Project Overview

This project explores how licorice (Glycyrrhiza glabra) was transformed from a naturally growing plant into a contested commercial commodity in the late Ottoman Empire.

Research Question

How did the commercialization of licorice reshape relationships between foreign companies, local actors, and the Ottoman state in Western Anatolia?

Why It Matters

By combining historical research with digital methods, this project visualizes the relationships behind licorice production and trade, helping explain how law, land, labor, and commerce became entangled in the late Ottoman period.
